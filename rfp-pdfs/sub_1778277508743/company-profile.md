Our Experience

World Wildlife Fund (WWF). Scoping a Reporting and Verification Platform for Place-Based Seafood Projects (2026–Ongoing). MarketEdge is supporting WWF to scope a credible, scalable reporting and verification platform for seafood jurisdictional initiatives, helping address a major gap in the current seafood sustainability ecosystem. The assignment is designed to assess platform pathways, stakeholder reporting needs, data governance requirements, and interoperability constraints so WWF and its partners can make informed decisions about how to structure a practical system for jurisdiction-level reporting and claims. MarketEdge is leading two integrated workstreams focused on platform feasibility and cost assessment, alongside stakeholder needs and adoption requirements, with the goal of identifying a viable reporting architecture that can support transparent, verifiable, and scalable place-based seafood improvement efforts.

The work combines structured evidence review, targeted consultations with platform operators and seafood system actors, comparative analysis of existing reporting and assurance systems, and AI-enabled synthesis through MarketEdge’s PartnerAI environment. Through this process, MarketEdge is assessing options to extend existing platforms, integrate across current seafood tools, and define the data, governance, verification, and operating conditions needed for real-world uptake. The engagement draws on MarketEdge’s expertise in seafood sustainability, digital platform analysis, multi-stakeholder engagement, and decision-ready strategy development, and will deliver recommendations that help WWF and partners reduce greenwashing risk, improve transparency, and strengthen the long-term credibility of seafood jurisdictional initiatives.



Palladium. UK-Ghana Partnership for Jobs and Economic Transformation (JET) Programme – Ghana: Pre-Feasibility and Comparative Site Analysis Textile and Garment Industrial Parks (2026–Ongoing)

MarketEdge, in partnership with Beyond Borders Development Group, is leading the pre-feasibility and comparative site analysis for Ghana’s proposed network of regional textile and garment industrial parks under the Government’s 24H+ industrialization agenda, supported by the UK-Ghana JET Programme implemented by Palladium. The assignment is designed to validate commercial opportunity, assess investor appetite, and prioritize sites based on real market demand and enabling conditions for sustainable sector growth.

The FCDO-funded engagement applies a commercially grounded, investor-informed lens to determine where parks are viable in the near term and what policy, infrastructure, workforce, and operational conditions are required to unlock long-term competitiveness. MarketEdge is leading market analysis, investor and tenant pipeline development, and comparative site assessment using its PartnerAI platform to accelerate data synthesis and structured stakeholder insights. Through coordinated engagement with government, manufacturers, developers, and industry actors, the team will deliver a transparent site ranking, identify early anchor prospects, and provide a capital-ready roadmap to sequence investment, mobilize private participation, and strengthen Ghana’s position within regional and global textile value chains.

DAI. FCDO COAST Program – Indonesia: Development of a National Mariculture Roadmap (2025–Ongoing).  MarketEdge has partnered with Indonesia’s Ministry of Marine Affairs and Fisheries under the FCDO-funded COAST Program to develop a National Mariculture Development Roadmap that guides a 12 million hectare expansion of marine aquaculture. The roadmap is designed to align national policy, private sector investment, and community priorities to strengthen coastal livelihoods, drive sustainable growth, and position Indonesia as a regional leader in low-carbon, climate-resilient aquaculture.

The assignment combines international benchmarking, policy and regulatory analysis, and extensive field engagement to ensure the roadmap is both evidence-based and locally owned. MarketEdge is leading an inclusive process that integrates surveys, focus group discussions, and in-person workshops across Indonesia’s coastal provinces, complemented by structured pause-and-reflection sessions that bring together government, industry, and community stakeholders. The resulting roadmap prioritizes short-, medium-, and long-term actions and investment pathways, providing a practical, government-led framework to advance Indonesia’s Blue Economy while delivering durable benefits for coastal people and ecosystems.

MarketEdge is supporting Nepal’s One to Watch in delivering the Nepal–U.S. Startup Weekend Challenge, a national accelerator-style initiative funded by the Public Affairs Section of the U.S. Embassy in Kathmandu.
